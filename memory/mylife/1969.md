# 1969

- Dave was probably still at Thornville, the junior school of Bradford Grammar School, for part or all of 1969; he thinks the Thornville period lasted about two years.
- The recurring memory is dislike/dread of the place, corporal punishment by cane, old-house atmosphere, odd-smelling books, and the daily walk to the main school for lunch.

- Possible transition year: dave thinks he moved from Thornville into the main Bradford Grammar School when he was about 11, probably around 1969 or 1970.
- He did the rest of his high school there.
- He remembers it as a strange, very formal place — “a bit like Hogwarts from Harry Potter.”
- Teachers wore black gowns and mortarboards, and the school had a highly formal atmosphere.
- Students wore funny brown uniforms and caps.
