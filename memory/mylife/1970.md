# 1970

- Dave thinks he moved from Thornville into the main Bradford Grammar School when he was about 11, probably around 1969 or 1970.
- He did the rest of his high school there.
- He remembers it as a strange, very formal place — “a bit like Hogwarts from Harry Potter.”
- Teachers wore black gowns and mortarboards, and the school had a highly formal atmosphere.
- Students wore funny brown uniforms and caps.

- During the Bradford Grammar School years, basketball was about the only team sport Dave ever played.
- Dave was pretty much always top of the class, but mischievous too.
- He had a good friend named John Hudson who lived in Keighley; they would hang out outside school, which felt a little unusual.
- Dave also hung out with Simon and Roger, who both went to Bradford Grammar School; Roger was in a different year, and Simon may have been in the same year.
- Mischief included smoking in the bathrooms, or at lunchtime in the large park across the street from school — Dave described it as Manningham Park / possibly “Nottingham Park.”
- The park was classic and old-fashioned, with a boating lake, bandstand, and a large hall/stately-home-like building that was a museum with a coffee shop.
- At lunchtime they would go across into the park, find a hidden place to hang out, and smoke cigarettes.
