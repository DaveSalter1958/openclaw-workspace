# 1980

- Left Wales after roughly a year, though it felt much longer.
- Converted his money into a Triumph Spitfire.
- Returned to Nottingham University as a research assistant.
- Worked for Pete Davis in traffic systems, counting cars, writing computer programs to identify vehicles, and developing work for Golden River, a company that made traffic sensors.
- Built systems using road sensors and computer programs to classify vehicle types from sensor patterns, such as trucks, two-axle vehicles, three-axle vehicles, and vans.
- Reconnected with Nottingham life, The Climbing Club, Dave Hughes, Andy Perkins, Sean Flattery/Hattery/Connery — Dave is unsure of the surname — and others.
- Wore memorable green stripy pants in at least one climbing photo Andy Perkins later sent.
- When first moving back, stayed with Vicki Casals, with whom he had been involved during undergraduate years; he does not think they slept together during that stay.
- Bought a house in Nottingham with help from his father, possibly for about £10,000, with each contributing roughly half.
