# Childhood Background

## Family and early sense of self — from Big Sur reflection, recorded 2026-05-16

- Dave described himself as starting out as a meek schoolboy / public-school boy in England.
- He grew up in a middle-class family that did not travel much; they were stay-at-home people.
- In his early world, family and community felt local and close, with people living within walking distance of each other.
- This restrained, local beginning stands in sharp contrast to the adventurous and international life he later lived.

## Sandal Primary School — early/mid 1960s, exact years uncertain

- Dave went to Sandal Primary School, starting around age five in about 1963.
- He remembers a teacher — possibly Mr Hansen — who had a pet monkey and sometimes brought his own chimpanzee into the classroom; this was extremely exciting to the children.
- At playtime, Dave played “Jackson Commandos.”
- He remembers flirting with Leslie Bates and kissing behind the trees behind the classrooms, possibly at a school field whose name he could not quite recall.
- He played soccer in the playground.
- Dave was promoted to Mrs Black’s Top Class a year early because he was considered very smart.
- Because of that, his parents decided he should take the Bradford Grammar School entrance exam — “where smart kids went.”

## Thornville / Bradford Grammar junior school — around 1968–1969

- After Sandal Primary, Dave thinks he went to Bradford Grammar School around 1968, first through its junior school, Thornville.
- Thornville was a separate old house with grounds on Manningham Lane.
- Dave's dad dropped him off there on the way to work, and Dave remembers really hating going in.
- He remembers the headmaster as a strange/funny figure who used the cane.
- Strong memories: being caned on the knuckles and backside, weird-smelling books, and walking each day to the main school for lunch along Manningham Lane and through a gate/path into the school grounds.
- Dave thinks he was there for about two years, but the memories are sparse and mostly atmospheric.

## Main Bradford Grammar School — from about 1969/1970

- Dave thinks he moved from Thornville into the main Bradford Grammar School when he was about 11, probably 1969 or 1970.
- He did the rest of his high school there.
- He remembers the school as strange and very formal, “a bit like Hogwarts from Harry Potter.”
- Teachers wore black gowns and mortarboards.
- Students wore funny brown uniforms and caps.

## Bradford Grammar School friendships, sport, and lunchtime mischief — early 1970s

- Basketball was about the only team sport Dave played in high school.
- He was pretty much always top of the class, but mischievous too.
- A good friend was John Hudson, who lived in Keighley; they hung out outside school, which Dave remembers as a little unusual.
- Dave also hung out with Simon and Roger, who both went to Bradford Grammar School; Roger was in a different year, and Simon may have been in Dave's year.
- Dave and friends smoked in the bathrooms, or at lunchtime in the large park across the street from school. Dave described the park as Manningham Park / possibly “Nottingham Park.”
- The park had a classic feel: boating lake, bandstand, and a big hall/stately-home-like building that was a museum with a coffee shop.
- Lunchtime pattern: cross into the park, find somewhere hidden to hang out, and smoke cigarettes.
