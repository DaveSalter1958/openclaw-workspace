# Undated / To Place Later

## Broad life arc from Big Sur reflection — recorded 2026-05-16

- Dave sees his life as a remarkable arc from a meek English public-school boy in a middle-class, stay-at-home family where everyone lived within walking distance, into a much broader and more adventurous life.
- He remembers branching out into rock climbing, caving, diving sumps in caves, scuba diving, skiing, a little ice climbing, and other outdoor/adventure pursuits.
- He was involved at the beginning of the development of rope access techniques and feels privileged to have helped shape an industry that later grew into a worldwide phenomenon.
- He later tried to transition into being a more conventional engineer after what he describes as a strange and non-standard engineering life, including not following a typical qualification path.
- He joined Keller and tried to operate as an engineer in a more conventional setting.
- He remembers a difficult period involving getting drunk, a DUI, and having to live in Lincoln at the castle while running a job under an unusual arrangement where he received some percentage of the profits.
- He sees moving to America as a major transformation: learning American systems, language, technology, units, culture, and ways of doing business.
- In America, he joined a business, became a significant and integral figure in it, and adapted to a new professional culture.
- Later life included moving across a whole state, getting divorced, raising kids alone, starting a business, building it successfully, developing a strong professional reputation, and eventually passing the business on so others could benefit from and grow it.
- Tone for later memoir: Dave is both astonished and grateful — "David, how you did all this shit" captures the self-surprised voice of this reflection.
